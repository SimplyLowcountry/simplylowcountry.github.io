What can I buy in Charleston, SC under $350,000?

Article by real estate agent Laura Street photograph by Cal Sigler Photography on July 9th 2020.

This week, Charleston was voted the #1 city in the United States by Travel and Leisure… again. That makes 8 years in a row that Charleston has not been ousted from its comfy seat in the top spot. With the recognition, brings a large number of people looking to relocate to the area. Whether they’re considering retiring, looking to start their professional career in the Holy City, or just looking for a change from high property taxes and less than desirable winters, Charleston often ends up on the short list.

One of the appealing things about Charleston is the lower property taxes, but also the cost of living, beaches, charming downtown area, and new industries moving in. When someone from New York calls me and is looking at properties online, they are often intrigued by what they would be able to purchase in the Charleston area under $350,000. Often times, they’re wondering if that is the norm for our market. While homes above $350,000 are plentiful, and demanded by some areas, there are numerous and amazing offerings on the market that would allow a buyer to stay within a $350,000 or less price point.

Last month, June of 2020, the top six selling areas in the MLS in the Charleston market, in terms of number of units sold were: West Ashley outside 526 (area 12), North Mount Pleasant (area 41), South Mount Pleasant (area 42), Summerville/Ridgeville (area 63), Summerville/Ladson/North Charleston-Dorchester (area 61) and Jedburg Road area of Summerville (area 74). Today, I’m breaking down for you what $350,000 can buy you in these top six selling areas and what makes them appealing to a buyer!

North Mount Pleasant

In June of 2020, 129 homes sold in North Mount Pleasant. North Mount Pleasant is considered the area to the north of the Isle of Palms connector, and often has the zip code of 29466 (although some properties are still classified as 29464).

North Mount Pleasant has a lot of new construction, and can really give you a “lot of bang for your buck” in the Mount Pleasant area. The closest beach is Isle of Palms, and that is anywhere from a 10 to 25 minute drive, just depending on what neighborhood you end up purchasing in. The grocery stores are plentiful, and the area has Target, Walmart and Costco nearby. There are numerous dining options, shopping options, boutique fitness studios and gyms, a library, hardware stores and more in North Mount Pleasant- meaning, that almost everything you need is going to be right at your fingertips.

So, what will $350,000 buy you in North Mount Pleasant?

South Mount Pleasant

With 122 sold properties in June of 2020, you guessed correctly if you guessed that South Mount Pleasant is considered anything south of the Isle of Palms connector. Just like north Mount Pleasant, the area has many grocery stores, restaurants, a post office and library, a brand new high school, gyms, fitness studios, a marina and again, a very short drive to your big stores like Target. While Isle of Palms could still be your closest beach depending on where you end up in south Mount Pleasant, Sullivan’s Island is also going to be another really close option for beach goers. If you live along the Coleman Boulevard corridor, Sullivan’s Island will definitely be your best bet for the beach, and it is even bike-able from some areas.

Summerville/Ridgeville

There were a total of 186 closed homes in the Summerville/Ridgeville area in June of 2020. There are many new construction options in the area, as well as 55+ communities, historic homes, and multi family properties such as townhomes and condos. The newest developments in the area, The Ponds and Summers Corner, bring things like a brand new YMCA, new schools, recreational offerings, and large planned communities with resort style amenities.

Jedburg Road area of Summerville

When we talk about really seeing the growth and development happening in Charleston, this area of Summerville is the best example of that. Communities such as Nexton and Cane Bay really show how much the area is growing, both in terms of housing and commercial real estate. Nexton Square now has restaurants such as Halls Chophouse, Taco Boy, and coming-soon Poogan’s Porch and Page’s Okra Grill. The area also has larger stores like BJ’s, Target, Walmart, and Belk. In addition, Summerville also has a quaint and charming historic Summerville downtown area with restaurants and local shops, and there are several schools, libraries, parks and quick access to I-26, as well as close proximity to Volvo’s new plant. Roper St. Francis also just opened a brand new hospital, the first in Berkeley County in 45 years!

North Charleston/ Summerville/ Ladson- Dorchester Area

125 closed sales in June of 2020, make the North Charleston/Summerville/Ladson- Dorchester area one of the top six selling areas in the Charleston area. Often times, home shoppers get confused when Summerville falls among 3 different areas, but the easiest way to explain it is by looking at a map. This particular area includes parts of North Charleston, Ladson, and Summerville that fall in Dorchester County and come up along the backside into Summerville. What makes this area so appealing is that you can purchase a great home at a great price, all while being a quick commute into downtown Charleston via Dorchester Road, and you’re located more centrally to North Charleston. The area has lots of shopping, dining, grocery stores, and every day conveniences. You’re also a short drive to the Charleston International Airport, and Boeing, Bosch and MUSC. Inventory in this area is a lot more sparse than others that we have looked at with only 61 homes on the market currently under $350,000.

West Ashely outside 526

West Ashley outside of 526 is known for its oak lined streets and natural beauty along Highway 61, new communities and development with great amenities, quick access into downtown Charleston and North Charleston, as well as the revitalization of the Citadel Mall area. With 144 sales in June of 2020, this area actually is ranked #3 on our list in terms of units sold. If you’re looking for a home in this area of West Ashley, there are 72 options.
